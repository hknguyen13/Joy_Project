Joy Project

  • Our Mission

    The Joy Project is a charity foundation that was founded in 2012 to better the lives of people in our community. It was inspired by a sermon at Southland Christian Church that emphasized the difference between moments of happiness and continuous joy.

    We believe that in order to achieve continuous joy one needs to unselfishly create joy for those around them. The Joy Project assists local members of the community through acts of kindness.

The Delgado's

The Delgado’s are a poverty stricken family from downtown Lexington. With a baby on the way they were struggling to find space for the new member of the family. This is when the Joy Project stepped in and donated valuable time and money to help out!

Karen

Karen is a friend of ours that works at Dunkin Donuts in downtown Lexington. At the age of 20 her daughter was murdered in the middle of the night. Exemplifying how donating one's time can make all the difference, The Joy Project reached out to Karen and visited her when she was in need.

Flipping Houses

Joe a local Lexingtonian who runs a business flipping houses was in need of help finishing his latest project on time. However, with The Joy Project on his side 15 volunteers managed to come out for a day and help finish construction, setting the project back on track!

Pete the Painter

Pete was once an alcoholic and homeless, but thanks to The Joy Project that is no longer the case!
